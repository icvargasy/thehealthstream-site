We map physiological networks, metabolic circuits, and behavioral protocols. Our objective is to make complex systems biology accessible, understandable, and actionable—bridging the gap between molecular pathways and daily lifestyle habits.

### 1. The Systems Biology Lens
We view the human body not as a collection of isolated symptoms, but as an interconnected circuit board of biological feedback loops. Every article on this registry maps these pathways upstream and downstream. By understanding the chemical signaling networks that govern our cells, we can make deliberate, evidence-informed decisions about our health span and longevity.

### 2. Epistemic Transparency
Longevity and health research sits at the frontier of science. To navigate this space responsibly, we employ a structured grading framework (adapted from the clinical GRADE system) to rate the quality of research supporting every entry. We map both consensus facts and frontier quests, making the distinction in data confidence immediately visible to the reader.

### 3. Absolute Independence
To preserve objectivity and serve our readers:
- **No Paid Endorsements**: We do not accept sponsorships to promote commercial health products or supplements. If a specific compound or product is mentioned, it is solely due to therapeutic or clinical relevance in peer-reviewed literature.
- **Open Access**: All compiled pathway decodings, jargon definitions, and clinical evidence reviews are entirely open-access and free of paywalls.
