Circadian Light & Sleep Optimization

#lifestyle#circadian#sleep

1-Minute Takeaway

Exposing eyes to bright sunlight within 30 minutes of waking and eliminating artificial blue light after dark aligns hormone release for deeper restorative sleep.

Epistemic Consensus Scale
High Controversy Developing Hypothesis Established Consensus

The suprachiasmatic nucleus regulates our circadian rhythm by using light cues to synchronize peripheral organs. Morning light resets this master clock, halting melatonin and triggering cortisol synthesis. During the day, waking activity builds up adenosine in the forebrain to create sleep pressure. Artificial light at night blocks melatonin, disrupting the deep and REM sleep cycles needed for neurological recovery.
