AMPK Cellular Energy Switch

#biology#metabolism#longevity

1-Minute Takeaway

Activating AMPK via periodic fasting, caloric restriction, or intense exercise shifts the cellular network from growth and storage into energy clearance and self-repair.

Epistemic Consensus Scale
High Controversy Developing Hypothesis Established Consensus

The AMPK (AMP-activated protein kinase) pathway is the body's metabolic energy sensor. When cellular energy (ATP) runs low, AMPK is phosphorylated. This turns on catabolic pathways to generate ATP and turns off energy-consuming anabolic pathways, like those controlled by mTOR. Snacking frequently keeps insulin high, which suppresses AMPK and locks the body in storage mode. Metabolic flexibility requires downregulating mTOR and upregulating AMPK to trigger cellular cleanup through autophagy.
