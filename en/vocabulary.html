Jargon Glossary Index

A precise index of biological, molecular, and longevity terms mapped across our pathway profiles.

AMPK

Adenosine monophosphate-activated protein kinase; a key enzyme regulating cellular energy homeostasis, activating cell cleanup processes.

Mentioned in: AMPK Cellular Energy Switch

adenosine

A cellular neuromodulator that accumulates during waking hours, generating homeostatic sleep pressure.

Mentioned in: Circadian Light & Sleep Optimization

autophagy

The natural, regulated mechanism of the cell that removes unnecessary or dysfunctional components, recycling cellular materials.

Mentioned in: AMPK Cellular Energy Switch

cardiorespiratory fitness

The capacity of the respiratory and circulatory systems to supply oxygen to working muscles during sustained exertion (often measured via VO2 max).

Mentioned in: Outlive: Science & Art of Longevity

circadian rhythm

The internal 24-hour biological clock regulating sleep, hormone release, and metabolic scheduling.

Mentioned in: Circadian Light & Sleep Optimization

healthspan

The period of life spent free from chronic disease, disability, and functional decline.

Mentioned in: Outlive: Science & Art of Longevity

mTOR

Mechanistic target of rapamycin; a protein kinase that acts as a central regulator of cell growth, proliferation, and protein synthesis.

Mentioned in: AMPK Cellular Energy Switch

melatonin

A hormone produced by the pineal gland that plays a key role in signaling nightfall and regulating sleep-wake cycles.

Mentioned in: Circadian Light & Sleep Optimization

metabolic flexibility

The capacity for the body to switch adaptively between substrate sources (carbohydrates and lipids) depending on physiological demands.

Mentioned in: AMPK Cellular Energy Switch

metabolic syndrome

A cluster of metabolic dysregulations (insulin resistance, high blood pressure, abdominal obesity) that elevate cardiovascular disease risk.

Mentioned in: Outlive: Science & Art of Longevity
